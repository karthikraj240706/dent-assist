🚨 Chairside Emergency Protocols

Immediate step-by-step actions for acute clinical complications.

1. Vasovagal Syncope (Fainting)

Action: Place patient in Trendelenburg position (head lower than feet). Maintain airway, administer 100% O₂, loosen tight clothing, monitor vitals.

2. Local Anesthesia Systemic Toxicity (LAST)

Action: Stop administration immediately. Call emergency help. Maintain airway, deliver 100% O₂. Administer 20% Lipid Emulsion therapy if available.

3. Anaphylactic Reaction

Action: Call EMS. Administer Epinephrine (0.3–0.5 mg of 1:1,000 IM in outer thigh). Deliver O₂ (10–15 L/min), elevate legs, monitor pulse.

4. Post-Extraction Hemorrhage

Action: Clear socket with suction. Apply firm pressure with sterile damp gauze for 30 min. Apply Tranexamic acid or gelatin sponge into socket if bleeding persists.
